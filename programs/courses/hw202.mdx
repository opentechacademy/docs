## Course Overview

HW202 covers the physical, electrical, and logical hub of the computer: the motherboard. You will learn to navigate the board layout, identify the appropriate headers for case connectivity, configure firmware settings (BIOS/UEFI), analyze chipset lane-sharing limitations, and methodically troubleshoot no-boot and no-POST failures.

---

## Course Outline

### Module 1: Motherboard Big Picture
Understand the motherboard as the map of the computer.
- **1.1 The motherboard as a city map**: Use a city map analogy to understand traces, sockets, DIMM slots, expansion lanes, and power delivery.
- **1.2 Quick Check: Motherboard map**: 3-question check on the function of the board, socket compatibility limits, and layout spacing considerations.

### Module 2: Ports, Headers, and Slots
Split external ports, internal headers, and expansion slots into simple groups.
- **2.1 Where everything plugs in**: Learn what USB headers, fan connectors, front-panel pins, SATA ports, M.2 slots, and PCIe expansions do.
- **2.2 Practice: Match a case to a board**: Autograded assignment requiring you to identify matching motherboard front-panel pins, USB-C headers, and fan ports for a specific PC case design.

### Module 3: Firmware and Startup
Learn what happens before the operating system loads.
- **3.1 Firmware is the startup rulebook**: Explain BIOS or UEFI, POST (Power-On Self-Test), boot order, and firmware settings.
- **3.2 Quick Check: Firmware and startup**: 3-question check on hardware initialization, CPU microcode compatibility, and using motherboard Debug LEDs to locate boot errors.

### Module 4: Chipsets and Shared Lanes
Make chipset features and lane sharing easier to understand.
- **4.1 Roads that sometimes share traffic**: Learn how motherboard chipsets manage data traffic, PCIe lane allocations, and lane-sharing limitations.
- **4.2 Practice: Read the footnotes first**: Autograded assignment testing your ability to read lane-sharing footnotes, select compatible storage configurations, and verify GPU clearance.

### Module 5: No-Boot Troubleshooting
Use calm first checks when a system will not start correctly.
- **5.1 Start with the front door**: Diagnostic checklist for no-boot, no-POST, and blank-screen scenarios.
- **5.2 Quick Check: No-boot basics**: 3-question check on power connections, clearing variables, and isolating memory modules during early diagnostics.

### Course Review & Final Exam
Review motherboard ideas and complete the final quiz.
- **6.1 Course review: What to remember**: Core concepts review on ports, slots, firmware, lane limits, and troubleshooting.
- **6.2 Final Quiz: Motherboard Deep Dive**: 5-question cumulative quiz testing your understanding of connection headers, BIOS updates, lane-sharing footnotes, and no-POST diagnostic habits.
